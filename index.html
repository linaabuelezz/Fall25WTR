Abstract / Introduction

This website details my experiences and challenges while working as a UI Software Developer at Candescent. I hope to share valuable insights on professional development,time management, teamwork, and achieving personal goals during my first work term at Candescent.

Employer

  • Candescent is a Waterloo-based fintech company that develops digital banking platforms used by financial institutions across North America; Their cloud-native, API-first solutions support secure online banking, account management, and data-driven insights. Not only does the company serve more than 1,300 banks and credit unions, but it also reaches over 30 million end users.
  • As a UI Software Developer at Candescent, I contributed to building intuitive user interfaces and frontend tools, applying computing science in the areas of web development, interface design, and software engineering for enterprise-scale applications.

Goals → Outcomes

Managing Time & Staying Organized

One of my priorities at Candescent was learning to manage my tasks effectively and meet deadlines without sacrificing quality. I adopted a personal tracker where I broke down larger projects into smaller deliverables and prioritized work based on urgency. Midway through each task, I’d check in with my team to confirm that I was aligned with expectations, which gave me confidence and saved me from rework later on. Daily progress reports during stand-up not only kept me accountable but also built trust with my team. By the end of the term, I had consistently met deadlines and had developed a reliable system that I will carry forward into future roles.

Becoming a Stronger Problem Solver

Debugging was an area I wanted to take to the next level. At the start of the term, I committed to tackling complex bugs by slowing down and understanding how each component interacted before jumping into a fix. For one particularly challenging bug, I mapped out the data flow across multiple files, which revealed a hidden dependency that had been overlooked. By approaching problems methodically and creatively, I was able to deliver a clean, effective fix without adding unnecessary complexity. This experience taught me the value of patience and systematic thinking, and it gave me more confidence in handling unfamiliar code.

Improving Written Communication

I recognized early on that good technical work can be undercut by poor communication, so I made a conscious effort to seek feedback and refine how I conveyed my ideas. I began documenting notes from every meeting, asking clarifying questions whenever I was given a task, and writing clearer pull request descriptions. One turning point was feedback suggesting I add more context to my PRs, such as the problem, approach, and testing notes. After applying this, my PRs became much easier to review, and I received positive feedback from teammates. These improvements not only helped my work stand out but also made collaboration smoother.

Contributing to Team Success

Beyond completing my own assignments, I wanted to ensure I was contributing positively to the team dynamic. I made a habit of checking in with colleagues to see if they needed help and offered to pair-program when someone was stuck. On two occasions, I directly helped teammates resolve blockers, which not only sped up our collective progress but also exposed me to new parts of the codebase. These experiences showed me the value of stepping in when others are struggling and reinforced the importance of building strong, supportive relationships on a team.

Mastering New Tools & Technologies

My personal learning goal was to deepen my understanding of Candescent’s tech stack. Each week, I set aside time to study documentation, READMEs, and wikis for tools I hadn’t used before. I asked teammates questions when I hit roadblocks and shared useful resources I discovered. Over the course of the term, I documented three new tools and libraries and applied them directly to my tasks. This not only made me a stronger contributor on my team but also gave me confidence in quickly picking up new technologies in the future.

Job Description & Highlights

At Candescent, I worked as a UI Software Developer using React to build and improve user-facing components. I integrated APIs, wrote tests, fixed bugs, and refactored legacy forms from Formik to React Hook Form (RHF). A key project I owned was creating functionality to warn secondary users that changes they made to an account would affect all users, which strengthened my skills in component design, testing, and API-driven development.

Conclusions

This work term at Candescent taught me how to deliver reliable code, communicate clearly, and work effectively with a team. I managed my time to meet deadlines, debugged complex issues with confidence, and improved the clarity of my documentation and pull requests. I also grew by helping teammates resolve blockers and by learning new tools that allowed me to own and ship a feature independently. If someone were to describe my experience, I’d want them to say: “She developed as a UI Software Developer who not only built strong technical skills in React and API-driven development, but also grew as a collaborator who communicates well and supports her team.”
